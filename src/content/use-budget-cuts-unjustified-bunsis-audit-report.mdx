  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> The analysis, completed by an accounting professor at Eastern Michigan University, is the first formal report to question the university’s <a class="link" href="https://www.provost.usc.edu/financial-planning-for-sustainable-excellence/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=crying-poverty-usc-budget-cuts-are-unjustified-third-party-report-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">claim</a> in November that it must slash spending to ensure “long-term financial resiliency.” </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> USC has since cut several popular programs — including <a class="link" href="https://morningtrojan.com/p/usc-restores-15-year-letter" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">free tuition</a> for the children of employees, select <a class="link" href="https://morningtrojan.com/p/usc-presidential-trustee-merit-scholarship-cuts" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">merit scholarships</a>, and <a class="link" href="https://morningtrojan.com/p/daily-trojan-budget-cut" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">funding</a> for the Daily Trojan newspaper — which has drawn <a class="link" href="https://morningtrojan.com/p/usc-planned-benefit-cuts" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">outrage</a> from students and employees. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “The university is crying poverty when there’s no reason to do so,” said <a class="link" href="https://www.emich.edu/cob/faculty/h_bunsis.php?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=crying-poverty-usc-budget-cuts-are-unjustified-third-party-report-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">Howard Bunsis</a>, the accountant who completed the analysis, in a webinar last week. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> USC’s chapter of the American Association of University Professors paid Bunsis for his work, a chapter spokesperson said. </p></div><style>

</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> The accountant has completed similar analyses at several other schools, most notably the California State University system. Previous universities subject to Bunsis’ scathing reports have <a class="link" href="https://www.buffalo.edu/news/key-issues/response-to-howard-bunsis-report.html?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=crying-poverty-usc-budget-cuts-are-unjustified-third-party-report-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">described</a> him as “a paid consultant with a pre-conceived agenda.” </p></div><style>

</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> The crux of USC’s justification for budget cuts is the fact that it ran on a $158 million operating deficit last school year, meaning it spent more than it made on core services like academic programs and the healthcare system. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> That calculation, however, excludes non-operating income like investment gains and donations to the endowment. All told, USC added over $600 million to its coffers last year, <a class="link" href="https://bpb-us-w1.wpmucdn.com/sites.usc.edu/dist/d/791/files/2024/11/2024-USC-Financial-Report.pdf?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=crying-poverty-usc-budget-cuts-are-unjustified-third-party-report-says" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">audited financial statements</a> show. </p></div><style>

  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> USC has blamed inflation, rising insurance costs, lowering graduate student enrollment, increasingly generous financial aid, and “the rising cost of college athletics” — among other things — for its deficit. </p></div><style>

</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> In its most recent financial statements, the university reported sitting on $11.7 billion of assets.   p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Three-fifths of USC’s assets are donor-restricted, which typically means the school has to wait until a designated time to spend the money.   p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> USC’s primary reserve ratio — a key measure of financial health that, in this case, is calculated by dividing unrestricted reserves by annual expenses — suggests that if all of the university’s revenue were to abruptly dry up, it could sustain itself for 6.5 months. </p></div><style>
