</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"><b>USC just enacted a significant</b> new policy<b> </b>that has flown largely under the radar. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> It’s a policy of “institutional neutrality,” buried nine paragraphs down in <a class="link" href="https://view.comms.usc.edu/?qs=c7c6581eef361236e93b2e977d9035af5ae76152ef900dea10bf19a3047fb5ba78db8c58af64c2975dad41fc948c2e9549d911afcc0b23099f2f0aaab55026fcd78c44b4637ba57084729dda71c613d4c1eff8614be93f22&utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=usc-just-enacted-an-underrated-free-speech-policy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">a recent university email</a>, that says USC and its leaders won’t take a public stance on any political, moral, or social issue unless it pertains directly to the school. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> This is notable. Institutional neutrality is a key tenet at schools like the University of Chicago, whose policies are generally regarded as the standard for protecting student free speech. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “The instrument of dissent and criticism is the individual faculty member or the individual student,” wrote University of Chicago legal scholar <a class="link" href="https://www.thefire.org/sites/default/files/2023/10/Kalven%20Report.pdf?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=usc-just-enacted-an-underrated-free-speech-policy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">Harry Kalven Jr.</a> in 1967. “The university is the home and sponsor of critics; it is not itself the critic.” </p></div><div style="font-size:0px;line-height:0px;padding:30px 0px 30px;"><div style="margin: 0 auto; border-top: 1px solid #c0c0c0; width:100%;"></div></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"><b>The argument for institutional neutrality </b>goes something like this: A university’s most important duty is to protect its students’ and faculty’s ability to exchange ideas freely. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> By taking a stance on a controversial issue, the university is bound to stifle debate — so it shouldn’t. But the key is that the school then can’t take a stance on <i>any </i>issue. </p></div><div style="padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;"><div style="padding-bottom:20px;padding-left:0px;padding-right:0px;padding-top:20px;"><img alt="" style="margin:0 auto 0 auto;width:100%;" src="https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/e26d09f0-df9d-4233-802c-4f4abf81ade5/state-of-univ-012.JPG?t=1725045419"/><div style="margin:0 auto 0 auto;padding:4px 0px 4px 0px;text-align:right;width:100%;"><small style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:12px;font-style:italic;text-decoration-color:#000000;text-decoration:none;"><p>USC President Carol Folt was widely criticized for her handling of last year’s pro-Palestinian protests. (Tomoki Chien / Daily Trojan)</p></small></div></div></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> For instance, imagine Congress passes bipartisan, wholly uncontroversial climate protections, and USC President Carol Folt makes an Instagram post celebrating the legislation. Innocuous enough. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> But then on Oct. 7, Hamas launches a bloody terror attack on Israel, igniting a war that will go on to kill tens of thousands of Palestinians. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Folt could choose not to make a statement. Supporters of institutional neutrality would argue she could inadvertently stifle university discourse — and is at the very least going to take flak for whatever she says. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Except, she already set the precedent that she’s willing to comment on social and political issues. If she could comment on the climate, why can’t she take a stance on Israel and Palestine? </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Her silence would say almost as much as an actual statement. So instead, she and her advisors have to wordsmith <a class="link" href="https://www.instagram.com/p/CyJPCcmRo3f/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=MzRlODBiNWFlZA==" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">a flaccid statement</a> that they hope won’t piss everybody off. And then <a class="link" href="https://www.instagram.com/p/CyPMeBCv7l_/?utm_source=ig_web_copy_link&igsh=MzRlODBiNWFlZA==" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">another one</a>, when it does. </p></div><div style="padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;"><div style="padding-bottom:20px;padding-left:0px;padding-right:0px;padding-top:20px;"><img alt="" style="margin:0 auto 0 auto;width:100%;" src="https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,format=auto,onerror=redirect,quality=80/uploads/asset/file/345aa358-8bba-460e-a42e-03ad84b1493a/palestine-encampment-5.jpg?t=1725045601"/><div style="margin:0 auto 0 auto;padding:4px 0px 4px 0px;text-align:right;width:100%;"><small style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:12px;font-style:italic;text-decoration-color:#000000;text-decoration:none;"><p>Los Angeles police arrested 48 students in last semester’s protests. (Tomoki Chien / Morning, Trojan)</p></small></div></div></div><style>

</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> The reality is that, no matter what activists on both sides say, the president of a posh Los Angeles university’s stance on Middle East relations is likely not going to stop Israel from dropping <a class="link" href="https://www.nytimes.com/2023/12/21/world/middleeast/israel-gaza-bomb-investigation.html?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=usc-just-enacted-an-underrated-free-speech-policy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">behemoth Mark 84 munitions</a> on Gaza, nor is it going to persuade Hamas to renounce its <a class="link" href="https://avalon.law.yale.edu/20th_century/hamas.asp?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=usc-just-enacted-an-underrated-free-speech-policy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">founding goal of killing Jews</a>. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> A supporter of institutional neutrality might say there are better things Folt could do with her time, namely ensuring that academic operations continue functioning as usual — and that students are free to civilly debate and exchange ideas. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> The policy does have its critics. Detractors have called it “<a class="link" href="http://institutional deception" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">institutional deception</a>” and a “<a class="link" href="https://www.mindingthecampus.org/2024/07/12/the-illusion-of-institutional-neutrality-a-mercifully-short-refresher/?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=usc-just-enacted-an-underrated-free-speech-policy" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">dishrag for cleaning up the occasional spill</a>.” They tend to argue that it’s impossible for a university to be neutral, and that such a policy is only a piece of paper for administrators to hide behind. </p></div><style>
