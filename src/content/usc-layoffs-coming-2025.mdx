</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Officials at academic units said they were scrambling over the weekend to balance their books. Many must submit budget proposals to the provost’s office this week — some as soon as Monday. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Schools must cut an average of 5% of their budgets while administrative units must cut 10%, internal documents show. Even under normal circumstances, those would be intimidating numbers. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> But because USC’s ongoing financial crisis has already eroded many non-personnel expenses, there’s not much left to cut, officials say. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> “You must be wasting a lot of money in materials and supplies, travel, and other expenditures to be able to find 5% of your overall budget in those categories,” said one person with knowledge of university finances. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Staff are expected to bear the brunt of the layoffs, though non-tenured faculty are also at risk “if they don’t find enough staff to lay off,” one official said.   p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Under USC’s financial structure, all schools — Dornsife, Viterbi, Keck, et al. — must divert a large share of their revenue to the university’s central administration to cover common overhead costs. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> Research-intensive schools might spend 35% of their revenue on these taxes, while a teaching-focused school might spend as much as 55%, according to the <a class="link" href="https://archive.ph/WGYTE?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=usc-layoffs-are-coming-sources-warn" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">Academic Senate</a>. The remaining money is generally what they can spend on school-specific expenses, like faculty salaries and supplies. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> The catch is that a 5% budget reduction is calculated using the school’s total revenue — not what it actually has left to spend after university taxes, one official said. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> If a school earns $1 million from tuition and other sources, a 5% reduction means it must cleave $50,000 in spending. But if half its revenue still goes toward university taxes, the school effectively has 10% less cash to work with. </p></div><style>
  p span[style*="font-size"] { line-height: 1.6; }
</style><div style="padding-bottom:12px;padding-left:10px;padding-right:10px;padding-top:12px;"><p style="color:#000000;color:var(--wt-text-on-background-color) !important;font-family:'Helvetica',Arial,sans-serif;font-size:19px;line-height:1.5;text-align:left;"> USC has seen several <a class="link" href="https://morningtrojan.com/p/live-usc-layoff-and-budget-cut-tracker#fundraising-communicators-september" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">layoffs</a> over the last year, though sources privy to budgeting discussions said they expect the imminent round to be more dramatic. One said that, under federal law, USC will likely have to file a <a class="link" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Worker_Adjustment_and_Retraining_Notification_Act_of_1988?utm_source=morningtrojan.com&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=usc-layoffs-are-coming-sources-warn" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" style="-webkit-text-decoration:underline #ac2124;color:#ac2124;text-decoration:underline #ac2124;word-break:break-word;;">WARN Act</a> notice, which requires large employers to notify employees 60 days ahead of a mass layoff. </p></div><style>
